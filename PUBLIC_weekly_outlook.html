Weekly Macro Theme

The dominant theme entering the week of 19 May is Dollar vulnerability. Last week's CPI miss and dovish Waller commentary have shifted the market's Fed narrative from "higher for longer" to "cut by September." This changes the fundamental backdrop for every USD pair on your watchlist.

The key question this week: will the FOMC Minutes on Tuesday confirm or contradict the dovish pivot the market has priced in? If the minutes show broad agreement that rates are sufficiently restrictive, Dollar selling will accelerate. If they show a divided committee, expect a short-term DXY bounce before the downtrend resumes.

Gold remains the cleanest long in this environment. Every pullback to $4,680–$4,700 is institutional accumulation. XAU/USD is not reversing — it is reloading.

Elijah's Weekly Thesis
This is a Dollar-selling week with a risk-managed approach. Do not chase moves — wait for the FOMC Minutes on Tuesday to set direction. The best setups will come Wednesday–Thursday after the dust settles. GBP/USD is the wildcard: the BoE on Thursday could either amplify or cancel the USD weakness trade.

How to Trade the Calendar
Two events dominate: FOMC Minutes Tuesday and BoE Thursday. Avoid new positions in the 2 hours before each release. Wait for the initial spike to settle — the second move after a major release is almost always the tradeable one. Do not trade the first 5-minute candle.
Scenario Planning — W/C 19 May 2026
FOMC Minutes — Tuesday 20 May
3:30 PM WAT · Maximum Impact
The minutes from the May FOMC meeting will either confirm or deny the market's dovish repricing. This is the single most important release of the week for USD pairs.
Dovish Minutes

Broad agreement that rates are sufficiently restrictive. USD selling resumes. EUR/USD targets 1.1350. Gold pushes toward $4,820. USD/JPY breaks below 153.80.

Hawkish Minutes

Divided committee or renewed inflation concern. USD bounces to 98.00. EUR/USD pulls back to 1.1150. Gold dips to $4,650–$4,680 before recovering.

BoE Decision — Thursday 22 May
12:00 PM WAT · Maximum Impact
The Bank of England is expected to hold rates at 5.0%. What matters is the language — specifically the vote split and Governor Bailey's press conference tone.
Hold + Hawkish Guidance

Bailey signals rates will remain elevated into year-end. GBP/USD breaks above 1.3340, targets 1.3420. GBP/JPY also catches a strong bid.

Hold + Dovish Language

Bailey opens door to cuts. GBP/USD drops to 1.3200–1.3220 zone. GBP strength thesis temporarily broken. Reassess GBP longs after the dust settles.

02
Hormuz Agreement = Oil Supply Signal
The summit produced one line that moves oil markets: both the US and China agreed the Strait of Hormuz must remain open. A joint diplomatic signal to Iran from the world's two superpowers introduces a small de-escalation premium into oil prices. If the Strait re-opens, energy-driven inflation eases, reducing Fed urgency.

Retail Sales Miss: Consumer Starting to Crack?
+0.5% vs +0.6% expected. Gas station spending slowed sharply. Furniture (-2%), cars (-0.5%), clothing (-1.5%), department stores (-3.2%) all fell. This is the first crack in the 'strong consumer = hot economy = Fed hikes' narrative. A Fed hike becomes harder to justify if spending is slowing.

Beat expectations. Small independent lift for GBP — the only major non-USD positive data of the day. Doesn't change the big picture (GBPUSD remains USD-driven) but prevented a deeper sell-off and sets up BoE dynamics next week.

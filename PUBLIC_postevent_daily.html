Week in Review

The week ending 16 May 2026 was defined by Dollar weakness and a flight into safe-haven assets. Gold pushed through $4,700 for the first time since February, while EUR/USD reclaimed 1.12 on the back of soft US CPI data and a shift in Fed language.

The key theme: markets are pricing out further Fed hikes. Two voting members signaled discomfort with the current rate path, and the CME FedWatch tool now shows a 72% probability of a cut by September. This changes everything for Dollar pairs in the coming weeks.

Retail traders were heavily long DXY at the start of the week — the squeeze that followed was textbook institutional behaviour. If you were watching the COT data, you saw this coming. If not, this week is your lesson on why positioning data matters.

Elijah's Take
The Dollar's weakness this week was not random. Smart money had been quietly unwinding long USD positions since late April. Retail caught on too late — by the time CPI dropped, the move was already 60% done. The lesson: the news is the exit, not the entry.
What Drove the Week
1
US CPI Came in Below Forecast
Headline CPI printed 2.3% vs 2.5% expected. Core CPI also missed at 2.8% vs 3.0% forecast. The immediate reaction was a 0.6% drop in DXY and a spike in Gold. EUR/USD jumped 80 pips in the first 15 minutes.
2
Fed's Waller Signalled Rate Cut Openness
Governor Waller, a voting member, stated the Fed "may have room to move" if inflation continues its descent. This was the dovish pivot the market had been waiting for. USD selling accelerated into the close.
3
Gold Broke $4,700 — Held the Level
XAU/USD pushed through and closed above $4,700 for the first time in 12 weeks. This is a significant structural break. The level now acts as support. A weekly close above confirms the next leg targeting $4,820–$4,900.

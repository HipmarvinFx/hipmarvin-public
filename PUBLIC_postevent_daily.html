Week Ending 18 May 2026
Analyst · Elijah Agom 19 May 2026 Free Edition
Week in Review

The week ending 18 May 2026 was defined by a brutal inflation shock and a complete repricing of Fed policy expectations. The Dollar surged nearly 1% on the week as both CPI and PPI came in far above consensus, ending all talk of rate cuts in 2026 and reviving serious discussion of a Fed rate hike.

The key theme: stagflation risk is back on the table. US CPI printed 3.8% y/y — the hottest reading since May 2023 — while PPI exploded to 6.0% y/y, a level not seen since 2022. The CME FedWatch tool has now fully priced out any cut this year, with a 40%+ probability of at least one hike by December. This is a fundamental regime shift for Dollar pairs.

Energy is the engine. The Strait of Hormuz remains closed, oil continues climbing, and supply-chain costs are bleeding upstream into consumer prices faster than the Fed anticipated. Retail traders long Gold and short the Dollar got caught badly this week. If you were watching the reflationary signals building since late April, the squeeze was not a surprise.

Elijah's Take
Everyone was positioned for cuts. That was the trade of H1 2026 — short Dollar, long Gold, long risk. This week the market got a face full of reality. A 6% PPI print does not allow a central bank to cut rates. It barely allows them to hold. The smart money that was quietly building Dollar longs since late April just got paid. The lesson this week is not about CPI — it is about not being married to a narrative when the data says something different.
What Drove the Week
1
US CPI Printed 3.8% — Hottest Since May 2023
Headline CPI came in at 3.8% y/y vs 3.7% expected. Core CPI also beat at 2.8% m/m annualised. Energy was the primary driver — Hormuz closure effects are now feeding directly into consumer prices. The Dollar bought immediately, Gold and EUR/USD sold off hard.
2
PPI Exploded to 6.0% y/y — Market Repriced Fed to Hike
The producer price print at 6.0% y/y and 1.4% m/m was the biggest shock of the week. Goods prices rose 2.0%, led by a 7.8% surge in energy. Transportation and warehousing costs surged 5.0%. CME FedWatch now prices a 40%+ probability of a rate hike by end-2026. This is not a soft landing story anymore.
3
Gold Broke Below $4,600 — Structural Shift in Progress
XAU/USD fell from the $4,700 area and closed the week near $4,533. Rising yields and a firmer Dollar crushed the gold thesis. The 20-day and 100-day SMAs have turned resistance. A clean break below $4,480 targets the $4,350 zone.

■ WHAT DROVE THE WEEK

The week of 12–16 May 2026 was the most macro-dense week of Q2. Four red folder events landed — US CPI (Monday), US PPI (Wednesday), Trump-Xi Summit Day 1 (Thursday), and Summit Day 2 + Warsh's first words as Fed Chair (Friday). The week's verdict: the USD won. DXY gained +1.4% across 4 straight sessions. USDJPY tagged 157.50, threatening the BOJ's intervention zone. EURUSD broke the 200-DMA and confirmed a medium-term bearish structure. The one surprise: AUD outperformed — the China risk-on proxy bid from the summit results was the best single trade of the week. Gold held its war floor at $4,700 despite heavy rate pressure. The consumer is beginning to crack (Retail Sales miss) but the Fed cannot act yet. Warsh arrives — and markets are watching every word.

■ THIS WEEK'S LESSONS — WHAT THE MARKET TAUGHT US
01
NEVER FIGHT A RED FOLDER TREND ON THE SAME DAY
CPI came in hot on Monday. Anyone trying to buy EURUSD or sell USDJPY on Monday got steamrolled. The market created a clear directional narrative at 8:30 AM ET — and that narrative dominated all day. The lesson: when a red folder event confirms a direction in the first 15 minutes, that direction is your bias for the session. Work with it, not against it.
02
INTERVENTION RISK IS A HARD STOP — NOT A CONSIDERATION
USDJPY at 157.50 threatened the BOJ's 158 intervention zone all week. Traders who sized up long above 157 made money — but were carrying massive tail risk. Japan has spent ¥10+ trillion defending the Yen. The rule for next time: take profits before 158.00 on USDJPY longs. Never hold large positions in a pair with active central bank intervention risk.
03
THE BEST TRADE CAME FROM THE SECONDARY CATALYST
AUD/USD was the week's best performer. It wasn't the primary event (CPI) — it was the Trump-Xi summit. The Hormuz agreement headline gave AUD an independent catalyst that had nothing to do with US inflation. The lesson: in any week with multiple catalysts, look for the pair that will benefit most from the secondary event. Often, that's where the best risk-reward lives.
04
REDUCE SIZE INTO THE WEEKEND — EVERY WEEK
Iran's ceasefire status could flip any day. The Trump-Xi summit produced surprises mid-week. Had either gone differently over a weekend, pairs would have gapped 200+ pips at Monday open. The rule is not new — but this week proved it: take profits or reduce size on all open positions by Friday close. Overnight geopolitical risk is non-negotiable to manage.
05
EARLY ENTRIES COST — WAIT FOR CONFIRMATION
The GBP/USD short was the right call — but the entry was one day early. UK GDP beat on Tuesday gave GBP an independent lift that hit the stop before the eventual move lower played out. If we had waited for Tuesday's close to confirm the bearish structure, the trade would have been a winner. Patience and confirmation beats early entries every single time.

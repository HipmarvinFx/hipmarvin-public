Week in Review

The week ending 16 May 2026 was a controlled, high-conviction week. Six setups were flagged. Four closed as wins, one as a loss, one as a partial close. Net result: +374 pips — one of the cleaner weeks of the year so far.

The dominant theme was Dollar weakness driven by a CPI miss and a dovish pivot from Fed Governor Waller. The two biggest winners — USD/JPY short and DXY long on the rebound — were both called before the events that triggered them. Preparation, not reaction.

The one loss came on GBP/USD short at 1.3501. The setup was valid structurally, but the Dollar weakness narrative overwhelmed it. The lesson: even technically correct setups get swept away when the macro tide shifts. Respect macro over structure.

Elijah's Weekly Note
Six trades. Four wins. One loss. One partial. That is not luck — that is a process. The loss on GBP/USD was not a mistake; it was a calculated risk that didn't pay off. A loss with a plan is infinitely better than a win without one. Keep your process tight going into next week — the FOMC Minutes will create noise. Let the noise settle before you trade.

Three Lessons from This Week
1
Process
Macro Context Overrides Technical Structure
The GBP/USD short was technically valid — but the macro environment made any USD-long a low-probability trade. Before entering any setup, ask: does this trade align with the dominant macro theme this week? If the answer is no, reduce size or skip the trade entirely.
2
From a Win
The News is the Exit, Not the Entry
USD/JPY and EUR/USD were both positioned before the CPI release — based on COT data and institutional flow. By the time CPI printed, the trade was already 60% done. Retail traders entered on the news. Smart money was already taking profit.
3
From a Loss
A Planned Loss is Not a Failed Trade
The GBP/USD loss of −46 pips was stopped out at the pre-defined level. The stop held. The trade closed cleanly. A loss with a plan is not a failure — it is the cost of doing business. Never move your stop to avoid a loss. Take it, review it, learn from it.
Going into Next Week
The FOMC Minutes on Tuesday will set the tone. Do not force trades Monday — wait for the minutes to give directional clarity. The best setups will appear Wednesday and Thursday. Patience is the edge most traders don't have.
